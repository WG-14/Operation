## Hypotheses to verify or falsify

### Hypothesis 1: The main weakness is evidence-layer confusion, not absence of expectancy metrics

Verify whether `strategy_performance.py` is an operational gate over closed `trade_lifecycles`, while research validation is a separate candidate approval path.

Check whether paper/live readiness evidence is currently validated mostly as artifact existence/hash/path custody rather than as semantically meaningful observation evidence.

Look for `profile-promote`, `profile-verify`, research promotion, paper validation, live readiness, execution-quality reports, strategy reports, experiment reports, approved profiles, and related tests.

Confirm or falsify whether profile promotion can pass with evidence artifacts whose internal observation window, decision count, closed lifecycle count, blocked decisions, execution quality breaches, unresolved orders, recovery blockers, or runtime/profile drift are not machine-validated.

### Hypothesis 2: Research/runtime decision equivalence is not yet fully proven

Verify whether the research engine is pure replay/simulation and intentionally separate from live broker/order lifecycle/run loop code.

Check whether the same approved profile can be used to prove that research-generated decisions and runtime/paper decisions are equivalent over the same candle snapshot and strategy parameters.

If equivalence is missing, implement or scaffold a deterministic decision-equivalence validation path.

The validation should compare, where possible:

* signal timestamps
* candle close/open basis
* entry/exit side
* strategy name
* parameter profile hash
* market and interval
* fee/slippage or execution model assumptions
* blocked/rejected decisions
* runtime decision telemetry

### Hypothesis 3: Overfitting controls exist, but experiment governance may still be incomplete

Inspect existing research manifests, walk-forward validation, final holdout handling, parameter stability checks, scenario policies, deterministic seeds, manifest hashing, candidate IDs, and report hashes.

Verify whether the repo records enough experiment-level governance to reconstruct:

* manifest hash
* dataset fingerprint/hash
* candidate count
* failed candidate count
* selected candidate
* promotion attempts
* holdout usage
* rerun/change history
* reason why candidates failed
* whether the best candidate came from a stable region or an isolated spike

If this is incomplete, design an append-only or content-hashed experiment registry/audit artifact that prevents cherry-picking and makes experiment lineage inspectable.

### Hypothesis 4: Live readiness needs semantic validation more than additional PnL metrics

Inspect how paper validation and live readiness evidence are supplied to profile promotion.

Verify whether evidence schemas enforce minimum observation days, minimum decision count, minimum closed lifecycle count, max blocked ratio, max execution-quality breach rate, no unresolved open orders, no recovery blockers, matching approved profile hash, matching DB/runtime fingerprint, and acceptable net/fee/slippage behavior.

If not present, implement typed evidence validation for paper and live readiness.
